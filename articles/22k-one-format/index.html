How I Built a 22K Audience With One Repeatable Format (and Why Consistency Beats Creativity)

Prajjwal Chittori · March 2026

In a few months, a single short-form format took me past 22,000 followers and into the millions of views — roughly 14.7 million in one 90-day window. I’m a backend engineer by day; this was a side experiment in distribution. The biggest surprise: the format mattered far more than any individual idea. Here’s the system.

The format: one shape, infinite content

Every video is the same skeleton:

  1. An English quote — from philosophy, a book, or a film.
  2. An explanation in Hindi — the meaning, unpacked plainly.
  3. A conclusion — one line that lands.

That’s it. Kafka, the 48 Laws of Power, literature, cinema — the content varies infinitely, but the container never changes. This is the whole trick, and it’s counterintuitive to creative people: a fixed container is a feature, not a limitation.

Why a fixed format wins

  • Production speed. When the structure is decided, you’re only ever sourcing content, never re-inventing the wrapper. I could ship 1–2 a day without burning out, because 80% of the decisions were already made.
  • Audience expectation. People don’t subscribe to a video; they subscribe to a promise. A consistent format is a promise the viewer can rely on, which is what converts a viewer into a follower.
  • The algorithm rewards consistency. Recommendation systems learn what your account “is” and who to show it to. Every off-format experiment resets that learning. Sameness, here, is leverage.

The niche was the other half

I sit at an unusual intersection — a crypto/payments engineer who actually reads philosophy — and the content lives at Hindi-language philosophy and self-help, a genuinely large and underserved distribution pocket. Pick a niche where your real, specific intersection meets a hungry audience. The format gives you velocity; the niche gives you a lane with room to run.

What the numbers actually taught me

14.7M views in 90 days sounds like a lot until you look at the next column: the vast majority were non-followers. That’s the short-form bargain — enormous reach, shallow conversion. The lesson for anyone doing this seriously: reach is not the bottleneck; the path from view to follower to something that matters is. I had distribution long before I had a way to convert it, and that order is backwards if your goal is anything beyond vanity metrics. Decide what a view is for before you chase a million of them.

If you’re starting

  1. Lock one format before your first post. Resist variety early; variety is a tax you pay in slower algorithmic learning.
  2. Find your real intersection. The thing only you can make is where two of your worlds overlap.
  3. Ship daily within the format. Volume inside a constraint compounds; volume across random formats doesn’t.
  4. Know your conversion goal up front. Reach without a destination is just noise you paid for with your time.

The creativity goes into what you say. The growth comes from never changing how you say it.
